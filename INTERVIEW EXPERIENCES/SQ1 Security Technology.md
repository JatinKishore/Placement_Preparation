# SQ1 Security Technology Interview Experience

The recruitment process at **SQ1 Security Technology** emphasizes a "Security Mindset" alongside practical expertise in network protocols, cloud environments, and endpoint defense. The process is designed to filter for proactive problem-solvers who can navigate the complexities of AI-powered defense.

---

## Key Aspects of the Interview Experience

### **1. Technical Screening (First Impressions)**
* **Baseline Knowledge**: Early conversations cover cybersecurity fundamentals such as the **CIA Triad**, **OSI Model** (focus on Layer 3 vs. Layer 4), and **TCP/IP**.
* **Practical Mindset**: You may be asked about your personal security habits—such as your choice of browser or search engine—to gauge your genuine interest in privacy and protection.

### **2. Technical & Domain Deep-Dive**
* **Security Protocols**: Expect in-depth questions on **VPNs**, **SSL/TLS encryption**, and **DNS**.
* **Attack & Defense**: Be prepared to explain the mechanics and prevention of **SQL Injection**, **Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)**, **DDoS**, and **Man-in-the-Middle (MITM)** attacks.
* **System Design**: For specialized roles, you might discuss **VPC/VNet Design**, **Active Directory**, or **SIEM** solution configurations.

### **3. Leadership & Perspective (CISO Round)**
* **Career Vision**: The final round with the CISO or senior management often moves away from "textbook" answers toward your **career perspectives** and situational judgment.
* **Adaptability**: Interviewers look for evidence of self-learning and how you stay updated on zero-day exploits and emerging attack techniques.

---

## Interview Rounds Summary
1.  **HR Screening**: Brief phone/video call to discuss your background and initial role suitability.
2.  **Technical Interview**: Deep dive into security fundamentals, networking, and practical scenarios.
3.  **Final Management/CISO Round**: Focused on cultural fit, work ethic, and long-term career perspectives.
