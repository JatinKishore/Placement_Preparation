# Thiran Technologies

## I. Thiran Technologies Interview Experience

The recruitment process at Thiran Technologies focuses heavily on logical thinking, problem-solving, and communication clarity.

#### 1. Group Discussion (GD)
- **Focus**: Communication skills and presence of mind.
- **Goal**: Initial filter to evaluate clarity of thought and team interaction.

#### 2. Written Assessment
- **Format**: Often includes an MCQ section on Microsoft Forms followed by a paper-based test.
- **Topics**:
  - **Aptitude**: Quantitative reasoning and verbal ability.
  - **Logical Reasoning**: Puzzles and analytical questions.
  - **Visual Logic**: Flowchart-based aptitude and "find the output" type questions.
  - [Click & Solve: Flowchart Aptitude Practice](https://www.scribd.com/document/373036859/Flowchart-Apptitude-With-Solution)

#### 3. Coding Round
- **Requirements**: Proficiency in Java, Python, C++, or JavaScript.
- **Key Concepts**:
  - **OOPS**: Classes, Methods, Inheritance, and Polymorphism.
  - **Data Structures**: Collections and Multi-threading.
  - **SQL**: Basic DB concepts and queries.
- **Sample Challenges**: Implementing specific logic for systems like a **Library Management System** or a **Maximo Consultant Challenge**.
  - [Click & Solve: Library Management System Design](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/design-library-management-system/)

#### 4. Technical Interview (Face-to-Face)
- **Tech Stack**: Deep dive into Java/Python fundamentals and SOLID principles.
- **Methodology**: Discussion on **Agile Delivery** and Scrum team experiences.
- **Project Walkthrough**: Explaining the strategic thinking behind your solutions.

#### 5. HR Interview
- **Focus**: Cultural fit, ownership, and long-term career planning within the "Thiran Family."
- **Check**: Professional growth aspirations and personal support needs.

  # Thiran Technologies Interview Experience

The interview process at **Thiran Technologies** for Trainee Software Engineer roles emphasizes logical reasoning, algorithmic proficiency, and presentation skills. The process is divided into five distinct stages, testing everything from manual logic drafting to public speaking.

---

## Key Aspects of the Interview Experience

### **1. Initial Screening (Pseudo Code & Flowcharts)**
* **Format**: Candidates must solve 10 pseudo code and flowchart problems.
* **Modality**: This round may be conducted in paper mode or online.
* **Objective**: It tests the ability to map out logical flows before writing actual code.

### **2. Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA)**
* **Problem Set**: Candidates typically face 2 DSA problems of Easy to Medium difficulty.
* **Common Topics**: Graph traversal problems are frequently prioritized in this stage.

### **3. Technical Discussion**
* **Focus**: Following the coding rounds, candidates engage in a technical discussion to explain their logic and core engineering concepts.

### **4. Presentation Rounds**
* **Group Presentation**: Assessment of teamwork and collective communication skills.
* **Solo Presentation**: A deep dive into the individual's ability to present technical or general topics independently.

### **5. HR Discussion**
* **Final Stage**: A final discussion with HR to cover cultural fitment and administrative details.

---

## Interview Rounds Summary
1. **Flow Chart & Pseudo Code**: 10 logic-based problems.
2. **DSA Round**: 2 problems, often involving graph traversals.
3. **Technical Discussion**: Deep dive into technical skills.
4. **Presentation**: Both group and individual sessions to test communication.
5. **HR Round**: Final interview and offer discussion.
