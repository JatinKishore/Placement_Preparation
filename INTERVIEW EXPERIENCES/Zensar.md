# Zensar Technologies - Interview Process

**Role:** Junior Software Developer (Velocity Program)  
**Recruitment Type:** Campus / Off-Campus  
**Process Location:** Virtual / On-Campus (Pune, Bengaluru, Hyderabad)  

---

## 1. Eligibility Criteria

- **Academic Performance:** Minimum **60%** in X, XII, and Graduation (No active backlogs).  
- **Qualification:** BE/B.Tech (CS, IT, ECE, EEE), MCA, or M.Sc (CS/IT).  
- **Year of Passing:** 2024, 2025, and 2026 Batches.  
- **Skillset:** Proficiency in **Java** and **SQL** is mandatory for the initial assessment.  

---

## 2. Job Details

### **Position:** Junior Software Developer  
- **Salary (CTC):** ₹4.0 LPA – ₹4.8 LPA (Base) | Specialized tracks up to **₹6.5 LPA**.  
- **Training:** Includes the **Velocity Program**, focusing on full-stack development.  

### Work Location:  
- Pune, Hyderabad, Bengaluru, or Chennai.  

---

## 3. Internship Details

- **Duration:** 3 to 6 Months (Prior to full-time onboarding).  
- **Stipend:** ₹15,000 per month.  
- **Focus:** Hands-on training in full-stack modules and participation in live shadow projects.  

---

## 4. Interview Process

The recruitment process consists of **4 distinct rounds**:

### 1. Online Assessment  
- **Format:** Technical MCQs + Coding.  
- **Focus:** Java fundamentals, MySQL query logic, and Quantitative Aptitude.  

### 2. Group Discussion (Optional)  
- **Format:** Communication-based filter.  
- **Topic:** General tech-business scenarios (e.g., "AI vs. Human Jobs").  

### 3. Technical Interview (35-40 Minutes)  
This round is known for high **breadth**, often asking 25-27 questions across various stacks:  
- **Java Core:** OOPS, `static`, `throw` vs `throws`, and the Diamond Problem.  
- **MERN Stack:** React Hooks (`useEffect`, `useState`), Virtual DOM, and MongoDB triggers.  
- **Databases:** MySQL Joins vs NoSQL (MongoDB) structure.  
- **Web Development:** HTML/CSS basics, DNS, and Web Protocols.  

### 4. HR Interview  
- **Focus:** Cultural fit, behavioral traits, and willingness to relocate/work in shifts.  

---

## 5. Technical Concepts & Logic

### **Java: Diamond Problem**
Java does not support multiple inheritance with classes to avoid ambiguity.


### **SQL vs NoSQL**
Be prepared to explain why you would choose MongoDB (for unstructured data/scalability) over MySQL (ACID compliance/relational data).


### **MERN Logic**
Explain how the Virtual DOM improves performance in React by batching updates to the real DOM.


---

## 6. Important Notes

- **Polyglot Expectation:** Even if you clear a Java assessment, be ready for MERN/JavaScript questions if they are on your resume.  
- **Communication:** Zensar places a heavy weight on how confidently you articulate your logic.  
- **Flexibility:** Must be willing to work in rotation shifts to support global clients (US/UK/AU).
