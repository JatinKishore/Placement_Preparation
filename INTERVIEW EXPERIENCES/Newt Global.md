# Newt Global Interview Experience

The interview process at **Newt Global** is often described as technical and thorough, focusing on a candidate's proficiency in cloud-native technologies, particularly **Java, AWS, and Python**. For senior roles, the process includes a final layer of client validation to ensure project alignment.

---

## Key Aspects of the Interview Experience

### **1. Initial Technical Screening**
* **Aptitude & Logic**: Some candidates face an initial aptitude or coding test to filter for logical reasoning and basic programming syntax.
* **Core Tech Evaluation**: The internal technical round is a deep dive into your CV. Expect scenario-based questions on **Java design patterns**, **AWS architecture (Lambda, S3, EC2)**, and **Python automation**.

### **2. Software Quality & Testing**
* **Testing Lifecycle**: For SDET or QA roles, Newt Global places high importance on **STLC (Software Testing Life Cycle)**, **Agile testing**, and automation tools like **QTP/Selenium**.
* **Deployment Logic**: Understanding the deployment process and how code moves through the **SDLC** is a recurring theme in technical discussions.

### **3. Client Interview (For Senior Roles)**
* **Project Alignment**: This is often the final hurdle. Clients evaluate your hands-on experience with specific tools and your ability to fit into their existing team structure.

### **4. HR & Culture Fit**
* **Work Logistics**: Be prepared to discuss your comfort with working from the **Chennai (Navalur)** office, as Newt Global maintains a strong local presence there.
* **Compensation**: The HR round is where salary negotiations and shift timings (given their global clients) are finalized.

---

## Interview Rounds Summary
1.  **Aptitude/Coding Test**: Preliminary screening of logic and programming basics.
2.  **Technical Round(s)**: 1-2 rounds focusing on Java, AWS, Python, and SQL.
3.  **Client Interview**: Specialized validation for senior or project-specific roles.
4.  **HR Interview**: Final discussion on salary, company policies, and cultural fit.
