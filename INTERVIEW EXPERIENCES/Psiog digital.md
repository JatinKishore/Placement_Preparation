# Psiog Digital Interview Experience

The recruitment process at **Psiog Digital** is a multi-stage marathon that evaluates a candidate's behavioral alignment, logical depth, and technical ownership. The process is unique for its heavy emphasis on **Use-Case Aptitude** and **Written HR Assessments**.

---

## Key Aspects of the Interview Experience

### **1. Multi-Sectioned Initial Screening**
* **Section 1 (Behavioral)**: 10 questions focused on cultural alignment.
* **Section 2 (Logical)**: 35 questions. Candidates have noted that resources like "Logically Yours" on YouTube are helpful for preparation.
* **Section 3 (Situational)**: 10 typed-answer questions where you must explain your approach to specific workplace scenarios.
* **Section 4 (Use-Case Aptitude)**: The most difficult section. It involves 2 large use-cases (e.g., **Calendar Logic**) with ~25 questions derived from them.

### **2. Advanced Aptitude & Technical Depth**
* **Advanced Aptitude (Non-Elimination)**: Solving complex use-cases on paper. This round allows the team to see your raw problem-solving steps.
* **Technical Round**: Highly resume-specific. Be prepared for deep dives into **React** (Hooks, State management) and **DevOps** (CI/CD pipelines, Docker) if listed on your CV. The panel will scrutinize your project and internship tech stacks.

### **3. Strategic Senior Developer Round**
* **The "Builder" Round**: A senior developer evaluates your architectural logic through live use-cases.
* **Common Scenarios**:
    - **Lift Algorithm**: How to optimize elevator movement for efficiency and wait time.
    - **Rating Systems**: Logical approach to ranking the "greatest movie of all time".

### **4. Written HR Assessment**
* **Self-Reflection**: You are given ~6 questions (e.g., "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?") which you must answer in writing before a verbal discussion.

---

## Interview Rounds Summary
1.  **Phase 1 Assessment**: 4-part online test (Behavioral, Logical, Situational, and Use-Case Apti).
2.  **Advanced Aptitude**: Paper-based use-case solving.
3.  **Technical Interview**: Project-centric deep dive into tech stacks like React and DevOps.
4.  **Written HR Round**: Answering 6 behavioral questions on paper followed by an interview.
5.  **Senior Developer Round**: Real-world problem solving (Lift algorithms, ranking logic).
